### Revenue Splits

**Venue Merch Cuts:**
- Range: 10-40% of gross merch sales
- Most common at club level: 15-25%
- Some venues: 0% for openers (merch cut only applies to headliner in some deals)
- Industry controversy: Bad Omens, Architects, Born of Osiris have all spoken publicly against venue merch cuts

**Management Cut:**
- 15-20% of gross revenue (industry standard)
- Typically applied to ALL revenue streams including merch
- On a $20,000 gross merch tour: $3,000-$4,000 to management

**Booking Agent Cut:**
- 10% of show guarantees (not typically applied to merch)

## Section 4: Risk Taxonomy

### The $30,000-$50,000 Claim — Forensic Analysis

Sam stated: *"if it doesn't go wrong, I could be out $30,000-$50,000"*

**Let's test this claim against the data.**

For Synestia as an opener on 21 dates:
- **Total theoretical merch revenue: $15,000-$25,000** (our mid-range estimate)
- Sam is claiming potential loss equal to or exceeding the **entire merch revenue** for the tour
- This is mathematically absurd unless he's conflating **headliner** economics with **his band's** economics

**If he's talking about the entire tour's merch across all 4 bands:** That might be $60-100K+ total. But Synestia's merch person is only handling **Synestia's** merch, not Shadow of Intent's.

**The claim doesn't survive contact with arithmetic.**

### Risk Categories — Quantified

| Risk | Probability/Show | Max Exposure/Incident | Experience Factor | Annual Industry Rate |
|------|------------------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Cash handling errors** | 10-20% | $20-$100 short till | Moderate — POS systems nearly eliminate this | $420-$2,100 over tour |
| **Inventory miscount** | 5-10% | $50-$200 (pricing confusion) | Low-moderate — pre-printed price lists fix | $52-$420 over tour |
| **Pricing mistakes** | 2-5% | $25-$100 | Low — price list + POS system | $10-$105 over tour |
| **External theft** | 1-3% | $50-$300 (grab-and-run) | Minimal — happens to anyone | $10-$189 over tour |
| **Stock-out on popular items** | 10-15% | $100-$500 (lost sales, not loss) | Moderate — experienced person anticipates demand | $210-$1,575 over tour |
| **Setup/teardown delays** | 5-10% | $50-$200 (missed early sales) | Moderate — familiarity helps | $52-$420 over tour |
| **Damage to inventory** | 1-2% | $100-$500 (water, crush) | Low — storage is storage | $21-$210 over tour |
| **POS system failure** | 2-5% | $100-$400 (cash-only fallback) | Low — tech failure is tech failure | $42-$420 over tour |
| **Left-behind inventory** | <1% | $200-$1,000 | Moderate — checklist eliminates | $0-$210 over tour |
| **Internal theft** | <1% | $100-$500 | Not experience-correlated | $0-$105 over tour |

### Total Risk-Adjusted Expected Loss from Inexperience

**Conservative (bad night every few shows):** $800-$2,000 over the full tour
**Realistic expected value:** $1,500-$3,500 over the full tour
**Absolute catastrophic worst case (everything goes wrong repeatedly):** $5,000-$8,000

**Compare to Sam's claim of $30,000-$50,000.** The realistic risk from inexperience is roughly **$1,500-$3,500** — or about **5-15%** of what Sam claimed. Even the catastrophic worst case is at most $8,000.

### What Actually Causes Large Merch Losses

The things that cause $30-50K losses have nothing to do with merch person inexperience:
1. **Merch inventory stolen from van/trailer** — thousands in product gone. This is a band-level security issue, not a merch person issue.
2. **Merch company overproduction** — ordering $30K of inventory that doesn't sell. This is an ordering decision, not an operational one.
3. **Venue merch cuts eating margins** — 25-30% venue cuts across 21 shows could take $5-7K. But this happens regardless of who's selling.

### Technology That Reduces the "Experience Requirement"

| Technology | What It Does | Cost | Risk Reduction |
|-----------|-------------|------|---------------|
| Square POS | Card payments, automatic totals, inventory tracking | ~2.6% per transaction | Eliminates cash math errors, creates audit trail |
| atVenu | Tour-specific POS with inventory management, settlement reports | Custom pricing | Industry-standard for professional touring, but expensive |
| Merch Cat | Inventory tracking, sales reporting | ~$10/month | Reduces count errors, tracks stock levels |
| Shopify POS | Inventory + payments integrated | $39/month + transaction fees | Familiar interface, easy training |

**Card vs. Cash trend (2024):** Tap-to-pay now more common than chip insert. Bands accepting cards see ~2x merch revenue vs. cash-only (Ari's Take). Most touring bands now run Square or similar.

**With Square or similar POS:**
- Cash handling errors → nearly eliminated
- Pricing mistakes → impossible (prices are in the system)
- Inventory tracking → automatic
- Audit trail → built in
- Training time → hours, not weeks

## Section 5: The Merch Person Question

### What Does an "Experienced" Merch Person Cost?

| Experience Level | Compensation | Per 21-Show Tour |
|-----------------|--------------|------------------|
| Friend doing a favor | Free + per diem + gas | $0-$500 |
| Inexperienced (Megan's deal) | ~$2,000 flat + tips + guest performance spot | ~$2,000 + value-in-kind |
| Entry-level professional | $50-$100/show | $1,050-$2,100 |
| Experienced professional | $100-$200/show | $2,100-$4,200 |
| Full-time tour merch (Glassdoor) | $63,680/year = ~$5,300/month | $5,000+ for 28 days |

### Megan's Package vs. "Experienced" Replacement

| Factor | Megan's Package | Experienced Hire |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Cash compensation | ~$2,000 + tips | $2,100-$4,200 |
| Additional value | Guest performance spot (exposure) | None |
| Motivation alignment | Invested in the scene, building career | Transactional |
| Risk premium | Needs training on specifics | Knows the routine |
| **Net cost delta** | — | **$100-$2,200 more** |

**The "experienced" merch person costs $100-$2,200 MORE than Megan's package.** For a band that's likely breaking even or losing money on this tour, that delta is significant.

### Training Options

| Option | Cost | Time | What It Covers |
|--------|------|------|---------------|
| Shadow a show (1-2 dates) | $0 | 1-2 days | Watch an experienced merch person, learn the flow |
| POS system training (Square) | $0 | 2-3 hours | Card processing, inventory tracking, end-of-night settlement |
| Price list + process doc | $0 | 1 hour | Prices, sizes, setup/teardown procedure, cash handling protocol |
| Trial run at a local show | $0 | 1 evening | Full dry run before tour starts |
| Professional merch training (if exists) | ~$200-$500 | 1-2 days | No widely-known formal programs exist for this niche |

**Total training investment to make Megan competent: $0-$500 and 2-3 days.**

### The Risk-Adjusted Analysis

| Factor | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Megan's compensation package | ~$2,000 + tips |
| Experienced hire cost | $2,100-$4,200 |
| Cost savings from Megan | $100-$2,200 |
| Expected loss from inexperience | $1,500-$3,500 |
| Cost of training to close gap | $0-$500 |
| **Net risk-adjusted delta** | **-$1,200 to +$1,200** |

**Translation:** The expected financial outcome of hiring Megan vs. an experienced person is roughly **a wash** — within $1,200 in either direction. The managers' framing of this as a $30-50K risk decision is off by an order of magnitude.

### What the Managers Aren't Saying

The real calculus isn't about merch competence:

1. **They may want to place their own person** — managers often have preferred crew. Keeping the crew in-house means loyalty and information control.
2. **They're applying headliner risk frameworks to an opener** — Shadow of Intent's merch might be $30-50K at stake. Synestia's is not.
3. **"Experience required" is a social gate, not a competence threshold** — there is no merch certification. "Experience" means "someone we already know."
4. **The girlfriend dynamic** — Sam admitted this was a sudden reversal after saying "time to book tickets." The timing correlates with relationship pressure, not operational analysis.
